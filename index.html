Hello! I’m Cyan, a computational physicist specialising in neutrino event reconstruction, machine learning, and high performance computing.

My latest research focuses on transformer-based models for high-energy neutrino flavour classification, focusing on IceCube neutrino event data. I have experimented with different architectural hyperparameters and their contribution to the final performance. I am certain there is plenty of room for further experiments.

I'm currently bringing this to a different context, assuming transformers can be a nice building block for a tiny regional weather forecast model.

My interests include:

  • Applying machine learning to scientific problems
  • Hardware optimisation for scientific problems
  • Data pipeline designing for scientific problems
  • Statistical representation of the data
  • High-energy (astro)particle physics

In this site you’ll find:

  • My points of view on ML and its application
  • Notes on physics, ML, and experiments
  • Personal thoughts and research reflections
